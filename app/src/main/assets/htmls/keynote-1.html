The Architecture and Critical Technologies of Space Information Networks

Abstract: Focusing on the main requirements and challenges in the space information network, an overall architecture of the space information network is proposed based on the national strategic planning and the present development status of the technologies after analyzing the characteristics of different space platforms. Meanwhile, the core scientific problems which need to be solved are expounded. In the end, a primary consideration of an integrated demonstration environment for key technology verification is presented.

Biography: Dr. Quan Yu, Academician of Chinese Academy of Engineering (CAE), was born in 1965. In 1982, Dr. Yu was enrolled in the Department of Information and Physics, Nanjing University. In 1986, he was enrolled in Xidian University after graduation. In 1988, he was selected to continue his study career in University of Limoges (France), by the Chinese government. He earned his Ph.D degree in Optical Fiber Communications in 1992. Dr. Yu was invited to serve as the Honorary Dean of School of Telecommunications Engineering, Xidian University in 2012. Currently, Dr. Yu serves as adjunct professor and Ph.D supervisor in Xidian University and Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

Dr. Yu was awarded First Prize of National Progress Awards in Science and Technology (once), Second Prize of National Progress Awards in Science and Technology (once), First Prize of ministry level Progress Awards in Science and Technology (4 times), Second Prize of ministry level Progress Awards in Science and Technology (twice), National Excellent Scientist Award (1997), Distinguished Young Scientist Award of China Association for Science and Technology (1999), Winner of Science & Technology Award for Chinese Youth (2006), Winner of China Youth May 4th Medal Pacesetter (2008), Winner of Science and Technology Award of the Ho Leung Ho Lee Foundation (2010). Dr. Yu has also filed 14 patents and published 7 books.
